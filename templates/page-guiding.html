Introduction to Tourism & Guiding

Overview of the global and regional tourism industry, emerging trends in eco and cultural tourism, the evolving role of tour guides as storytellers, cultural ambassadors, and safety managers. Explore how changing traveler expectations (authentic experiences, sustainability) shape guiding practices.

Tour Design & Itinerary Planning

Principles of route mapping and logistics, methods for balancing travel time with guest enjoyment, creating flexible itineraries to accommodate diverse groups, budgeting techniques for transport, meals, and attractions. Includes vendor negotiations, supplier management, and contingency planning for weather or transport delays.

Destination Knowledge & Cultural Awareness

In-depth study of cultural heritage, historical landmarks, natural attractions, and local traditions. Learn to research destinations accurately, avoid cultural misrepresentation, and incorporate storytelling that respects indigenous knowledge and heritage. Examine case studies on sensitive tourism issues and responsible visitor behavior.

City Heritage Walking Tour (Individual)

25%

Purpose: Assess individual guiding ability, interpretation, and storytelling through the delivery of a city heritage walking tour.

Task: Individually research, plan, and conduct a 30-minute guided walking tour featuring selected heritage sites. Demonstrate knowledge of cultural and historical context, apply effective guiding techniques to engage the audience.

Themed Immersive Experience (Team)

25%

Purpose: Assess design and delivery of a packaged themed tour (e.g., culinary trail, eco-walk).

Task: In teams of 3-4, design a 2-hour themed tour package, produce a marketing one-pager, safety plan, costed itinerary, and deliver a 20-minute key segment of the tour to an assessor panel.

Accessibility & Inclusive Guiding (Practical + Written)

50%

Purpose: Demonstrate competency in adapting tours for diverse needs.

Task: Adapt an existing 60-minute tour to be accessible for participants with mobility impairment and sensory differences. Provide annotated route map, adapted script, equipment checklist and deliver a 10-minute demonstration segment.

50% Accessibility & Inclusive Guiding (Practical + Written)

The assessment structure for the Guiding Certificate is designed to evaluate both theoretical knowledge and practical application across three key components.

Assessment Breakdown

  • City Heritage Walking Tour (25%): Individual practical assessment demonstrating cultural knowledge and engagement techniques
  • Themed Experience (25%): Team-based project combining planning, marketing, and delivery of a specialized tour
  • Accessibility & Inclusion (50%): Combined practical and written assessment focused on adaptive guiding for diverse needs
